Clean Water and Sanitation Affordable and Clean Energy

One in three people in the world do not have access to safe drinking water.

As you can imagine, this is a rather large problem.

Sanitation is also a big issue, with four out of ten people without access to basic handwashing. That's around...

...three billion people unable to use proper sanitation.

Water is an essential resource. Not only do you need it to live, it's also needed for poverty reduction, food, ecosystems and education. It's important to ensure that everyone has equal access to water.

So what can you do?

Start in your own home. Take short showers instead of baths. Reduce the amount of meats, like beef, pork, chicken, etc. that you eat. Make sure to completely close faucets when not in use.

The most effective thing you can do is raise awareness. Tell your friends and family about the importance of water, and let them know what they can do. Send letters to your local mayor. Participate in World Water Day and World Toilet day. We need to work together in order to improve our future.

Further Reading

Three billion people use electricity from wood, coal, charcoal or animal wastes.

This is a pretty big problem, if you also consider that 60% of greenhouse gas emissions come from electricity production.

So, what's the solution?

At home, reduce the amount of energy used. Turn off appliances that aren't in use, like lightbulbs, and invest in more efficient devices. While they may be more expensive, the money saved from the less electricity used will pay off in a few years.
